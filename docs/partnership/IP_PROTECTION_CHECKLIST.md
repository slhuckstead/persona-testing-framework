### 3. File Provisional Patent Application (Within 2 Weeks)

**Cost:** $2,000-3,000 (DIY) or $5,000-8,000 (with attorney)

**Why provisional:**
- Locks in your filing date (critical for "first to file" patent law)
- Gives you 12 months to develop and find partners
- Allows you to say "Patent Pending"
- Much cheaper than full patent ($15K-25K)

**How to file:**

**Option A: DIY (Save Money)**
1. Visit uspto.gov/patents/basics/apply/provisional
2. Use your invention disclosure as basis
3. Include all specification documents
4. Fee: $75-300 depending on entity size
5. Attorney review: $2,000 (optional but recommended)

**Option B: Attorney (Recommended)**
1. Google: "patent attorney [your city] software"
2. Interview 3 attorneys (most give free 30-min consultation)
3. Ask: "Experience with software/UX patents?"
4. Cost: $5K-8K for provisional
5. They handle everything

**Recommended attorneys to contact:**
- Find via: American Intellectual Property Law Association (AIPLA)
- Look for: "Software patents", "User interface patents"
- Red flag: If they've never done software patents

**Timeline:**
- Week 1: Contact 3 attorneys
- Week 2: Choose one, provide materials
- Week 3-4: Attorney files provisional

**What happens:**
- You get patent application number
- Can claim "Patent Pending"
- Have 12 months to file full patent
- Your invention date is PROTECTED

- Attorney: $500-1000 for custom NDA

**Use mutual NDA (both parties protected):**
```
MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

   - Multi-Dimensional Persona Testing Framework concept

   - Business plans, partner strategies, market analysis

2. Obligations:
   - Receiving Party will not disclose to third parties
   - Will not use for own benefit without written permission
   - Will protect with same care as own confidential info

3. Term: 2 years from date of disclosure

4. Exceptions: Public information, independently developed

[Signatures]
```

**Action:** Have this ready BEFORE approaching anyone.

### 5. Copyright Registration (Optional but Good)

**Cost:** $65 online at copyright.gov

**What to register:**
- The specification documents (as "literary work")
- Any code you write (as "software")
- Documentation, diagrams, flowcharts

**Why:**
- Provides legal proof of authorship
- Stronger protection in disputes
- Cheap insurance ($65)

**How:**
1. Visit copyright.gov/registration
2. Select "Literary Work" or "Computer Program"
3. Upload PDF of your documents
4. Pay $65
5. Get certificate in 3-6 months
