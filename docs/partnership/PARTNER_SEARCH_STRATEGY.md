### Equity Split (Industry Standard for Idea + Engineer)
**Recommended: 50/50 split**
- You: Inventor, CEO/CPO (Chief Product Officer), owns IP
- Partner: CTO (Chief Technology Officer), builds product
- Both: Co-founders with equal stake

**Why 50/50 is fair:**
- Ideas without execution = $0
- Execution without novel idea = commodity
- Together = valuable company
- Equal partnership = equal commitment

**Alternative splits if you contribute more:**
- 60/40 (you/them) if you: raise money, do sales, manage business
- 55/45 if you: contribute some funding, have customers lined up
- 70/30 only if you: have funding, customers, AND they're employee not partner

- ‚ùå "Let's start, we'll figure out equity later" - Always fails

## ü§ù Vetting Potential Partners

### First Conversation (30 min)

**Your goals:**
1. Do they understand the problem?
2. Are they excited about it?
3. Do they respect your role?
4. Can they explain technical concepts simply?

**Questions to ask:**
1. "What's your background in testing/UX?"
2. "What excites you about this problem?"
3. "How would you approach building this?"
4. "What concerns do you have?"
5. "What's your experience with open source?"
6. "Have you done a startup before?"
7. "What's your ideal working relationship?"

**Red flags:**
- "This is easy, I can build in a weekend" (oversimplifying)
- "Ideas don't matter" (won't respect your input)
- "Let me take this and think about it" (might build alone)
- Can't explain things simply (won't be good collaborator)

**Green flags:**
- Asks lots of questions about the problem
- Suggests improvements to your framework
- Talks about "we" not "I"
- Proposes working agreement upfront
- Shares their own ideas openly

### Second Meeting (After NDA Signed)

**Share:**
- Full specification documents
- Technical requirements
- Roadmap and timeline
- Business model details

**Discuss:**
- Division of responsibilities
- Equity split
- Vesting schedule (4 years, 1 year cliff)
- Decision-making process
- IP assignment

### Trial Period (Before Committing)

**Option:** 1-month paid trial
- Pay: $3,000-5,000 for month
- Deliverable: Architecture document + proof of concept
- Both evaluate: Do we work well together?

**If works well:** Convert to equity partnership
**If doesn't work:** Part ways, you own the code

## üìã Partnership Agreement Essentials

### What to Include (Get Attorney to Draft)

**1. Equity Split**
- You: 50%, vesting over 4 years
- Partner: 50%, vesting over 4 years
- 1-year cliff (if quit in first year, get nothing)

**2. Roles & Responsibilities**
- You: CEO, Product, Business Development, Fundraising
- Partner: CTO, Engineering, Technical Architecture

**3. IP Assignment**
- Partner assigns all work to company
- You assign framework IP to company
- Both sign: "All work belongs to company"

**4. Decision Making**
- Major decisions: Both must agree (deadlock = outside arbitrator)
- Day-to-day: Each owns their domain

**5. Vesting**
- 4-year vesting, 25% per year
- 1-year cliff (nothing if leave before 1 year)
- Accelerates if company acquired

**6. Exit Scenarios**
- If one quits: Equity stops vesting
- If fired: Depends on cause (with/without cause)
- If company fails: Equity worthless (that's startup risk)

**Cost:** $2,000-5,000 for attorney to draft
**Worth it:** Prevents 90% of co-founder conflicts

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Sharing before NDA**
- Always get NDA signed first
- Even if they seem trustworthy
- Protects both of you

**Mistake 2: Verbal agreements**
- Everything in writing
- Equity, roles, decisions
- "Let's figure it out later" = failure

**Mistake 3: Unequal partnership with "boss" mentality**
- You're partners, not boss/employee
- Respect their expertise
- Listen to technical concerns

**Mistake 4: Rushing due to excitement**
- Date before marrying
- Work together 1-2 months before equity
- Co-founder breakup is worst startup killer

**Mistake 5: Choosing friend over qualified partner**
- Friend with wrong skills = failed startup + lost friendship
- Better: Qualified stranger who becomes friend
